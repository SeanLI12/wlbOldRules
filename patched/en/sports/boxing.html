BOXING

Last Update: 02/09/2019

General Rules
  • Upon the bell sound signaling the start of the first round, all bets will stand.
  • The duration of each round is 3 minutes; 90 seconds will be used to determine the "half" round stated in over/under markets.
  • Should there be any changes in the stipulated number of rounds, all over/under bets will be considered void and refunded.
  • For Head to Head (H2H) bet types, if the match ends in a draw, bets on both boxers will be considered void.
  • Bets will be settled based on the results declared in the ring which can be determined by scorecard points, TKO, KO or disqualifications.
    • KO (Knockout) includes TKO (Technical Knock Out) and disqualifications of a boxer.
    • Draw includes technical draw.
    • Decision includes technical decisions.
  • Revision or amendments made to the results after the boxers leave the ring will not be considered.
  • Should there be a withdrawal or a substitution of the listed boxers, all bets will be considered void.
  • If it is announced that either or both of the boxers fail to meet the specified weight for the advertised contest, all bets on that match will be considered void.


FIGHTING (MIXED MARTIAL ARTS)

General Rules
  • All bets will be considered valid once an announcement indicating the start of round one has been made by the referee.
  • For UFC, every round lasts 5 minutes; 150 seconds will be used to determine the "half" round stated in over/under markets.
  • Each title match has five rounds, and non-title matches have three. There is a one minute rest period between rounds.
  • The results obtained at the end of the match are considered final, which will include points, submission, TKO, KO or disqualification. Revision or amendments made to the results after the fighters have left the ring will not be considered valid.
    • Submission: a fighter clearly taps on the mat or his opponent or verbally submits.
    • Technical Knockout (TKO): If a fighter cannot continue, the fight is ended as a technical knockout.
    • Draw includes Technical Draw.
  • In events where there are any recounts of the judges' scorecards or changes made by governing authorities after the match, such changes will be considered invalid for settlement purposes.
